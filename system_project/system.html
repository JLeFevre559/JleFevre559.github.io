The planets are set on a logarithmic scale so that Jupiter's radius is 3x the radius of Earth, making the sun's radius ~8.44x the radius of Earth.
To see the relative scale to Earth of each planet on this scale you can use this equation:


The default speed multiplier of 1 represents the planets moving 1 hour per real second.
Currently the planets are moving approximately 1 hour each real second.
At this speed the planets are moving about 3600x as fast as real life!
At this speed Earth will complete a full orbit in approximately 146 minutes.

Adjust this multiplier below.

Hint: change the speed to 0.00027778 to see the planets move at close to real time.
